Holistic Well-Being & Self-Care

Self-care, for me, is not a checklist or a trend; it is a survival skill and a professional responsibility. My ability to offer grounded, compassionate, and ethical care depends on how well I tend to my own mind, body, and spirit.

The Ocean as a Grounding Place

The ocean has always been my anchor. I visit often because I relate to its changing character—soft and gentle one day, fierce and determined the next. The ocean mirrors my lived experience and reminds me that strength and vulnerability can coexist.

Being near the water helps me think through difficult decisions, process emotions, and reconnect with myself and the wider world. It is a place of restoration, clarity, and perspective.

Integrative Health & Yoga

My MA in Integrative Health & Healing exposed me to natural and energy medicine, psycho-neuroimmunology, spirituality and health, and a wide range of complementary therapies. My thesis focused on auricular acupuncture for service members with polytrauma syndrome, reflecting my interest in trauma-informed, somatic approaches to healing.

As a Registered Yoga Teacher trained in Germany, I use yoga as both a personal and professional tool—supporting nervous system regulation, embodiment, and gentle, accessible movement. My practice emphasizes non-harm, balance, and compassion, values that also guide my work with patients and families.

Everyday Practices That Sustain Me

  • Walking outdoors in all seasons, especially near water
  • Gentle yoga, stretching, and breathwork
  • Journaling and reflective writing
  • Setting boundaries around time and emotional energy
  • Seeking supervision, mentorship, or therapy when needed
  • Staying connected with my children and close friends

These practices support my longevity in practice and my ability to show up with presence, clarity, and compassion.

Selected Academic Work & Assignments

The assignments highlighted on this page represent coursework that felt especially aligned with my interests in health systems, social determinants of health, global models of care, and equity for military families. Each piece contributed to my evolving understanding of how policy, culture, and lived experience intersect.

Stakeholders in Global Healthcare Systems

Format: Academic paper

This paper examines how governments, insurers, healthcare organizations, and communities contribute to the design and delivery of care in different parts of the world. It compares national models with attention to equity, access, financing, and long-term sustainability, and reflects on how these structures impact patient and family experiences.

Social Determinants of Health: America vs. Germany

Format: Academic paper

Drawing on both research and lived experience as a military spouse in Germany, this paper explores how social, economic, and policy conditions shape health outcomes in the United States and Germany. It considers housing, employment, social protection, and family supports as core influences on health and well-being.

View Paper (DOCX)

Quality, Safety & Policy Making

Format: Academic paper

This paper connects personal experiences of giving birth overseas with broader concepts of quality and safety in healthcare. It analyzes how gaps in TRICARE Overseas postpartum coverage affect military families and proposes policy directions to support continuity of care, maternal mental health, and early intervention.

Reva Rubin’s Theory of Maternal Identity

Format: Presentation

This presentation examines Reva Rubin’s work on maternal identity formation and its relevance for contemporary nursing and mental health practice. It reflects on how nurses, midwives, and advanced practice providers can support parents in navigating the emotional and psychological transitions of early parenthood.

Evidence-Based Practice & DNP Research Questions

Format: Academic paper

In this assignment, I explored how evidence-based practice informs advanced nursing roles and the development of clinically relevant research questions at the DNP level. The work helped clarify my interest in studying enhanced postpartum care models for military families, particularly as they relate to mental health and social support.

Expanding Equity for Military Families Overseas

Format: Video presentation

This recorded presentation addresses systemic barriers faced by TRICARE beneficiaries stationed abroad, with a specific focus on perinatal and postpartum services. It outlines opportunities to build more equitable, culturally sensitive, and accessible modes of support and serves as a conceptual bridge in the development of MOB Family.

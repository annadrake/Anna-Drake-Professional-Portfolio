Global Outreach & Medical Anthropology

My professional practice has been shaped by fieldwork and service in the Dominican Republic, Ghana, and Peru. Each setting offered a different perspective on how geography, history, culture, and economics influence health and access to care.

Dominican Republic – Rural Mountain Clinics

In the Dominican Republic, I worked with an interdisciplinary team and the Peace Corps to provide care in remote mountain communities. Travel across rugged terrain was often required to reach families whose access to healthcare was limited and unpredictable.

Ghana – Fertility and Nutrition Education

In rural Ghana, I collaborated with the NGO Global Mamas and accompanied U.S. Military Academy cadets during cultural immersion training. I designed and delivered maternal health education focused on fertility and nutrition.

Peru – Indigenous Healing Traditions

In the Quechua region of Peru, I learned from traditional Andean healers known as Hampiq, whose work centers ritual, prayer, and reciprocity with Pachamama (Mother Earth).

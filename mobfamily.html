Military Overseas Birth & Family Center (MOB Family)

MOB Family grew out of my lived experience as a military spouse raising children in Germany, navigating pregnancy, birth, and postpartum care within both TRICARE Overseas and the German midwifery-led model. It is, at its heart, an effort to bring the best of these worlds together on behalf of families who often feel unseen.

In Germany, postpartum home visits from midwives are standard. After each birth, a midwife came to my home to assess my healing, support breastfeeding, check my newborn’s weight and well-being, and offer reassurance. This gentle, relational care contrasted sharply with what many American families—and especially military families—receive. It felt like being “mothered” as I mothered my children.

Mission

MOB Family exists to strengthen postpartum and family support for U.S. military families stationed overseas by:

  • Advocating for enhanced, culturally sensitive postpartum care within TRICARE Overseas
  • Providing education and resources for parents navigating host-nation systems
  • Fostering community among military families experiencing pregnancy and early parenting abroad
  • Highlighting evidence-based models of care, including midwifery-led and home-based support

Who We Serve

MOB Family primarily serves:

  • Pregnant and postpartum military spouses living overseas
  • Families adapting to new cultures, languages, and healthcare systems
  • Parents navigating deployments, reintegration, and the demands of early parenting
  • Those at risk for perinatal mood and anxiety disorders who need early, sustained support

Germany as a Living Classroom

Living in Wiesbaden turned my everyday life into a kind of ongoing fieldwork—observing how German midwives, obstetricians, and community supports surrounded families in the perinatal period, and how American systems either aligned or fell short. These contrasts became the foundation for my desire to transform postpartum care for military families.

Vision

I envision TRICARE Overseas as a place where ideas can be tested, refined, and humanized—a living laboratory for postpartum care that respects cultural wisdom while honoring modern standards of safety and continuity. By beginning with a defined population—military families stationed abroad—we can pilot, evaluate, and refine approaches that may eventually inform domestic insurance systems as well.

MOB Family is one expression of that vision: a bridge between systems, cultures, and the everyday realities of parenthood.

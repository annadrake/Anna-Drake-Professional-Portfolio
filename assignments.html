Selected Academic Work & Assignments

These assignments represent academic work that I found especially meaningful during my BSN studies and related coursework. They highlight my interests in health policy, global systems, social determinants of health, and equity for military families.

Stakeholders in Global Healthcare Systems

Format: Academic paper

An analysis of how governments, insurers, health systems, and communities influence care delivery around the world. The paper compares different national models with an emphasis on equity, access, and sustainability.

Social Determinants of Health: America vs. Germany

Format: Academic paper

A comparative exploration of how social, economic, and policy environments shape health outcomes in the United States and Germany. This work weaves together lived experience as a military spouse in Germany with research on structural supports, chronic stress, and family well-being.

Quality, Safety & Policy Making

Format: Academic paper

This paper connects personal experiences giving birth overseas with concepts of quality, safety, and policy reform. It examines how gaps in TRICARE Overseas postpartum care impact military families and considers how policy could better support continuity and maternal mental health.

Reva Rubin’s Theory of Maternal Identity

Format: Presentation

A theoretical analysis of Reva Rubin’s work on maternal identity formation, with reflections on how nurses, midwives, and mental health providers can support parents in the vulnerable transition to motherhood.

Evidence-Based Practice & DNP Research Questions

Format: Academic paper

An exploration of how evidence-based practice informs advanced nursing roles and the development of research questions at the DNP level. This assignment helped clarify my interest in studying enhanced postpartum care models for military families.

Expanding Equity for Military Families Overseas

Format: Video presentation

A recorded presentation outlining systemic barriers faced by TRICARE beneficiaries stationed abroad, particularly around perinatal and postpartum care. It proposes strategies for more equitable, accessible services and laid the early foundation for MOB Family.
